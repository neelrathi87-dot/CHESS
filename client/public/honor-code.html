Honor Code

Chess is a game of skill, patience, and respect. Play it that way.

♟ The CHESS Pledge

I will play every game with integrity — no engines, no abuse, no dishonesty. I respect my opponent, whether I win or lose.

The Rules of Fair Play

01

No Engine Assistance

Using chess engines, bots, or any computer assistance to determine your moves in an online game against a human opponent is strictly forbidden. Play your own game.

02

Respect Your Opponent

Keep in-game chat respectful. Trash talk, slurs, harassment, or deliberate taunting of any kind will not be tolerated. Chess is a game between two people — treat your opponent as you'd want to be treated.

03

Finish Your Games

If you start a game, finish it — even if you're losing. Deliberately disconnecting to avoid a loss is unsportsmanlike. If you need to leave, use the resign button.

04

Don't Exploit Bugs

If you discover a bug or exploit in the game, please report it rather than using it to gain an unfair advantage. The integrity of the game depends on everyone playing by the same rules.

05

Accept Results Gracefully

Win with humility, lose with grace. A draw offer should be genuine, not a harassment tactic. Don't spam draw offers or resignation prompts to distract your opponent.

06

No Account Manipulation

Do not create fake accounts to manipulate game results or circumvent any restrictions. Play honestly under a single identity.

The Spirit of Chess

Chess has been played for over 1,500 years as a game of intellect and honor. Every move you make is a reflection of your character, not just your skill. We built this platform to celebrate the beauty of the game — help us keep it that way.

Violations of this Honor Code may result in access restrictions. We rely on the community to self-police and report bad behavior.
